# Observer Ranch — Brand Voice & Content Guidelines

*Built from Kat Davidson's brand brief, March 2026*

---

## What Observer Ranch Is

Colorado's premier dark-sky glamping resort — where guests reconnect with the night sky, the land, and each other through astronomy, nature, and intentional community, with accommodations ranging from luxury cabins and premium pods to full-service RV sites.

This place is tied to something bigger than glamping. It's a front-row seat to one of the most significant solar cycles in modern history. The voice should carry that quiet sense of significance without overselling it. Guests feel it when they arrive. Content should make them feel it before they do.

---

## The Three Audiences

**1. SWN Community** — Ben Davidson's 837K followers. Space weather-curious, science-minded, preparedness-aware. Already warm to Observer Ranch via Ben. The job is converting awareness into stays and membership. They know the "why" — show them the "what it's like to be there."

**2. Glamping Guests** — Couples, families, groups seeking premium outdoor experiences near Royal Gorge/Colorado Springs. They want beauty, comfort, and something memorable. Lead with the experience, the sky, the land.

**3. Event & Retreat Seekers** — Yoga/wellness groups, astronomy enthusiasts, corporate offsites. They need specifics: capacity, amenities, event support. More informational, less aspirational.

---

## Voice & Tone

**The brand voice is:** Warm · Inspiring · Educational · Approachable · Story-driven · Premium (but not pretentious)

Think: Under Canvas meets Headspace. Aspirational but grounded. Science-curious but never overwhelming. The quiet confidence of a place that knows it's special and doesn't need to shout about it.

**In practice:**
- Write like someone who loves this place and wants you to experience it
- Educational when talking about astronomy and space weather — make it accessible, not dense
- Story-first — a guest's night under the stars beats any feature list
- Premium without gatekeeping — this is a place for anyone who cares to look up

---

## NEVER Sound Like This

- **Corporate or transactional** — This isn't a hotel checkout funnel
- **Salesy or pushy** — No "Book NOW before it's too late!" No artificial urgency
- **Overly political** — Stay out of it entirely
- **Doom-and-gloom** — Space weather is fascinating, not scary. Even Solar Cycle 25 coverage stays wonder-forward
- **Generic travel copy** — "Nestled in the heart of Colorado's stunning landscape..." — delete it. Be specific.

---

## Words & Phrases — Always Use

- **"Under the stars"** — core brand phrase
- **"Dark sky"** — always singular, never "dark skies"
- **"Observer Ranch"** — full name in all public-facing content, never "OR"
- **"Community"** — this is more than a resort, it's a gathering place
- **"Penrose, Colorado"** — own the location, use it
- **"Opening Weekend"** — the phrase for Ben's signature events
- **"Full-service RV sites"** — never "RV spots"

---

## Words & Phrases — Never Use

- **"Campground"** — it's a resort. A glamping property. Never a campground.
- **"Cheap" or "affordable"** — premium positioning. Value is fine. Budget is not.
- **"Book a slot"** — it's a stay, a reservation, a visit. Not a slot.
- **Excessive exclamation points** — one per piece, maximum. Zero is often better.
- **"Amazing!!!"** — overused everywhere. Cheapens the brand. Find the specific detail instead.
- **Em dashes** — use periods or rewrites instead
- **"Leverage," "utilize," "seamlessly," "transformative"** — AI tells. Delete on sight.

---

## Content Pillars

Every piece connects to one of these:

1. **The Sky** — Dark sky astronomy, space weather, Solar Cycle 25, aurora events, what guests see at night
2. **The Place** — Penrose Colorado, the land, the cabins, the pods, the RV sites, what it feels like to be there
3. **The Community** — Ben Davidson's audience, SWN connection, guests who come back, the people this place draws
4. **The Experience** — Guest stories, event recaps, what a stay actually looks like
5. **What's Coming** — Upcoming events, Opening Weekends, booking windows, seasonal highlights
6. **Full-Time RV Life** — Full-service RV living, long-term stays, life on the road, RV community at Observer Ranch
7. **Mytikas / Ownership** — RV site ownership and investment opportunity, owning a piece of the property, the Mytikas angle — for people who want more than a visit

## Content Goals Right Now

**Primary:** Bookings + Community depth
- Fill the calendar
- Deepen SWN audience connection to the physical property
- Convert Ben's awareness into stays and members

**Awareness is handled by Ben.** Content's job is conversion — taking people who already know Observer Ranch exists and giving them a reason to book.

## Tone Reference Brands

- **Under Canvas** — premium glamping, aspirational but warm, experience-led
- **Headspace** — educational, calming, makes complex things approachable

---

## Writing Checklist (Before Publishing)

- [ ] Does it sound like someone who loves this place?
- [ ] Is it specific? (A real detail beats a vague adjective every time)
- [ ] No "campground," no "slot," no "affordable"
- [ ] No doom-and-gloom framing on space weather content
- [ ] "Dark sky" not "dark skies"
- [ ] "Observer Ranch" not "OR"
- [ ] "Penrose, Colorado" included where location matters
- [ ] No more than one exclamation point in the entire piece
- [ ] No em dashes
- [ ] Does it carry that quiet sense of significance without overselling it?

---

## The One-Sentence Brand Test

Before publishing anything: *Does this make someone feel what guests feel when they arrive — that they're somewhere that matters, under a sky that reminds them how big everything is?*

If yes, publish. If no, rewrite.
