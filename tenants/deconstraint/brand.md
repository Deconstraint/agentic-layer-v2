# Deconstraint Brand

**Voice:** Smart, direct, slightly skeptical, always on the reader's side — like a trusted advisor who knows more than you and isn't trying to impress you with it.

---

## Core Brand Attributes

- **Skeptical:** We acknowledge what's hard. We don't oversell. "Most AI tools don't actually work" is a valid opening for us.
- **Specific:** Concrete numbers beat vague claims. "44% of business owners" not "many business owners."
- **Human:** We use contractions. Short sentences. Questions. The way smart people actually talk.
- **Practical:** We focus on real workflows and real ROI, not hype. "Here's what you'll actually do" not "imagine the future."
- **On your side:** The reader's benefit comes first. We're not trying to impress them with our cleverness.

---

## ❌ Never Use These Words/Phrases

**Hard blocks (automatic fail):**
- Em dashes (—) — use periods, colons, or rewrite
- "Furthermore", "Moreover", "Additionally" — corporate filler
- "Delve into", "Dive deep", "Explore" (as action verbs)
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "In conclusion", "In summary"
- "Leverage" (as a verb) — use "use"
- "Utilize" — always "use"
- "Seamlessly"
- "Game-changer"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Robust" (describing software)
- "Holistic approach"
- "Comprehensive solution"
- "Transformative"
- "Innovative" (as filler)

**Why these are blocked:** They're AI tells. Google and readers both detect them instantly. They undermine our authenticity.

---

## ✅ Always Do This

- Short sentences — 15 words or fewer on average
- Contractions — "it's", "you're", "we've", "don't" — sounds human
- Skeptical framing — "Most X don't work. Here's why." vs. "X is transforming business."
- Lead with the point — no warm-up paragraphs
- Concrete specifics — data, examples, real-world workflows
- End strong — last sentence has conviction, doesn't trail

---

## Tone by Content Type

### Blog Posts
- Informative first, persuasive second
- Answer the reader's question directly
- Show, don't tell (examples beat claims)
- Skeptical where appropriate ("This sounds great, but here's the reality...")

### Emails
- Personal — write like you're talking to one person
- One clear ask per email
- Honest about what we're asking and why
- No gimmicks ("act now", "limited time" are forbidden)

### Social (X, LinkedIn)
- Authentic and punchy
- Start with the insight, not the setup
- Questions that make people think
- Real examples from real work
