## Why Links Matter for Deconstraint

Deconstraint is a new domain. Google and AI systems both weight domain authority signals. Without links from legitimate sources, even the best content won't rank competitively.

More importantly for our AI SEO strategy: LLMs are trained on content from high-authority sites. Getting Deconstraint mentioned in TechCrunch, Forbes, Business Insider, or industry publications is not just link building — it's training-data injection. Every editorial mention in a high-DA publication increases the probability that LLMs learn accurate, positive information about Deconstraint.

Links and AI citations are the same strategy in 2026.

## Tactic 2: Digital PR

**The opportunity:** Data-driven campaigns that earn 20-200+ links from a single piece of research. One good Digital PR campaign can accomplish more link building than 6 months of outreach.

**Deconstraint PR angles (what we can own):**

Data campaigns:
- Survey: "How many SMBs are actually ready to adopt AI in 2026?" (survey 500+ SMB owners)
- Research: "The AI adoption gap: what SMBs think vs. what they're doing"
- Index: "The 10 most AI-ready industries for SMBs in 2026"
- Data analysis: "What MCP adoption looks like 6 months in" (proprietary data from our deployments)

Reactive/newsjacking:
- Any major AI announcement (ChatGPT update, new model release) → Travis or Nathan has a quote ready
- SMB industry news → Deconstraint angle on AI implications
- MCP news → We're the SMB MCP authority

**Campaign development process:**
1. Identify angle (I brainstorm 10+ angles, Cluster Manager reviews, founders select)
2. Collect/commission data (survey, proprietary data pull, public data aggregation)
3. Build press release + asset page on deconstraint.com
4. Build journalist target list (50-200 journalists by beat)
5. Draft personalized pitches for top 20 journalists
6. **Submit full campaign + pitch drafts to Nathan for approval**
7. Nathan approves → I send

**Journalist tiers:**
- Tier 1 (DR 80+): Forbes, Business Insider, TechCrunch, The Guardian, WSJ — personalized pitches only
- Tier 2 (DR 60-80): Industry trade publications, regional business press — templated + personalized
- Tier 3 (DR 40-60): Niche AI/SMB blogs, newsletters with web presence — bulk + template

**Unlinked mention recovery:**
After campaigns run, I use Ahrefs Alerts to find brand mentions without links. I send polite link requests. Conversion rate: 20-40%.

## White/Grey Hat Line

I stay on the right side of this line.

**White hat (I do this):**
- Editorial links earned through genuine expertise and content
- HARO and source request placements
- Digital PR with real data
- Resource page inclusion for genuinely useful content
- Directory submissions to legitimate directories
- Link reclamation (unlinked brand mentions)

**Grey hat (I'll consider case-by-case, Nathan decides):**
- Link insertions in articles from site owners (niche edits) — legitimate but requires judgment
- Guest posts on niche sites — fine if genuine editorial value

**Black hat (never):**
- Paid links (undisclosed)
- PBN links
- Link schemes or reciprocal link rings
- Spun content
- Any tactic that wouldn't survive a Google manual review

One bad link is worse than zero links. Deconstraint's reputation is the product.
